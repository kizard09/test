Readers' Stories

Many invited me to their church or their civic group to share the message of believing in ourselves and in something bigger. In each city, I met people trusting their own whispers and Doing Good. Some dreams were as big as a building and others were making a difference one life at a time. Meeting these inspiring people and finding out was calling them is part of my next chapter. Here are a few of the many I have met. If you know someone who has listened to their whisper to find their calling let me know about it. Email me their (or your) photo and story: Email Kathy

Caroline Bundy

Birmingham, AL

As Development Director for AIDS Alabama, Caroline has a day job. But she could not shake the idea that what she needed to be working on was a shelter for homeless and runaway youth to protect them from being caught up in sex-trafficking or other dangers of the streets. When we met at Canterbury United Methodist, I felt like she was at the beginning of the exact same journey I had just been through with Moore Place. Caroline is now listening to her whisper and developing a community plan for a shelter for at-risk youth near the city-center of Birmingham.

Caroline Hart

Crossnore, NC

I have received a lot of emails since publishing but none like Caroline’s. She wrote, “I've been hearing the whisper for some time, but afraid to listen - it's scary, daunting and will change my life forever.” That whisper? To adopt a sibling set of four children to add to their family of one son. She added: “It's not hundreds of homeless, but I sure hope by loving these four as our own, we will change the trajectory of their precious lives.” The Hart adoption will be final in 2018 taking their family from three to seven!

Caroline Chambre Hammock

Kankan, Guinea

You might recognize Caroline’s name from the book as the first director of Moore Place. Caroline was with UMC until last year when she kept hearing whispers about returning to West Africa where she had once worked for the Peace Corps. Caroline tells me that reading my book amplified those whispers so she finally listened. Caroline is on a six-month assignment for the World Food Programme in Guinea. You can read about her amazing adventures https://guineangirl.wordpress.com

Melissa Leahy & Rachel Estes

Birmingham, AL

Rachel shared my book in her little free library and Melissa was one of the early readers. After many email exchanges, we finally met in March 2017 when Rachel invited me to speak at Canterbury United Methodist Church. Rachel is a FORCE for good, leading her church’s mission and outreach, creating a social justice book club for kids, mom to her own daughters and oh yeah, just started a doctoral program!

Gigi Dobbins

Charleston, SC

Gigi and I met at her church’s Advent luncheon organized by Chase Wood for St. Philips Episcopal Church. Gigi helped sell books at the luncheon but then asked if she could sell them in her store in Charleston, Rhodes Boutique. Gigi has now sold well over one hundred copies of The Hundred Story Home and as far as I know is the only dress store retailer in the country selling my book!

Mike Ruckart

Charlotte, NC

A friend in my book club, Ann Temple couldn’t wait to tell me about her last haircut. She sat down with her hairstylist, Mike, and he said, “You have got to read this book!” It was The Hundred Story Home. Mike and I had never met, but he had been lending out copies to all his customers. When I went to meet him and thank him, he asked if I was going to write another book. I told him the Moore Place miracle was all I had to tell right now and he said, “Well, make something up—I want to read more!” Thanks, Mike—working on some more nonfiction!

Kathy Hess Paquette

Fort Mill, SC

I had never met Kathy when she emailed to see if I would speak at her church South Mecklenburg Presbyterian in South Charlotte. I would soon find out what a “doer” she is not only holding discussion groups about The Hundred Story Home but also organizing a large speaking event which raised enough for 150 gift cards to make the holidays bright for Moore Place residents. And she introduced me to her identical twin sister in Austin, Texas. See story on Peggy Hess Greenawalt.

Peggy Hess Greenwalt

Austin, TX

If Peggy reminds you of someone in another reader story—you are right. Peggy and Kathy Paquette are identical twins and both are church librarians. After speaking at her sister’s church, Peggy invited me to Austin for an incredible gathering in her home. Her friends not only raised over $600 that night for Moore Place, Peggy presented me with a special glass jar with “Moore Place Fund” inscribed on it for future events.

Beth Duckett

Winston Salem, NC

Beth wrote to me after her book club read The Hundred Story Home, “I think we could have talked all day about you, homeless issues, mental health issues…and more.” They decided to share the message so Beth invited me to speak at Centenary United Methodist Church. Over 150 people came and with great marketing by Rosemary Pulliam, they sold almost two hundred books! If I had a Beth and Rosemary in every city, this book would hit the NY Times best seller list!

Marylin Taylor

Winston Salem, NC

And then she wrote that Eugene Coleman had done odd jobs for them for years but the Taylors had moved and lost track of Coleman and worried something had happened. Then Marilyn wrote: “When I read about him in your book, I woke up my husband, shouting, "I found Eugene Coleman!” The Taylors have reconnected and took Coleman to lunch. Eliza Phillips

West Chester, PA

Eliza and I met in a God-instance that probably takes a whole chapter of another book to explain! But she invited me to Christ Church Christiana Hundred in Wilmington, Delaware. Chase Wood

Charleston, SC

Chase invited me to my first out of town keynote speech and I will always appreciate her faith in me! The event was for her ECW Advent Luncheon at St. Philip’s Church in historic Charleston. Dozens of women came to celebrate the season and purchased four cases of books for Christmas gifts!

Knox Barker

Greensboro, NC

Knox invited me to Holy Trinity Episcopal Church for a women’s dinner and to speak with their Prayers in the Park group which ministers to the homeless in Greensboro. Only after the incredible sold-out dinner did I find out that Knox has a great business baking and delivering these amazing coffee cakes with fresh flowers— which I now order and send as my go-to gift. Beth Hardin

Charlotte, NC

As vice chancellor for business affairs, Beth Hardin is UNC Charlotte's chief financial and administrative officer. But she is also a huge bibliophile and loves recommending good reads. Beth leads a discussion group at Holy Comforter Episcopal Church and has shared the book there and all over town. Beth has purchased more than fifty books Park Road Books and is still giving—including with her hairdresser who now shares the book with her clients!

Kristan Seaford

Matthews, NC

It’s hard to imagine I had never met Kristan until my book brought us together. An avid fitness instructor and runner, Kristan had to find a whole new way to live after a catastrophic illness struck in 2013. After 100 days, Kristan survived a medically-induced coma but awoke to realize she was now a triple amputee. Today, Kristan is a mother of five children, counselor, motivational speaker and blogger. And I can’t wait for her to finish her book! Her inspirational story www.kristanseaford.com

Meg Robertson

Denver, NC/Buffalo, NY

Meg wrote me after receiving this copy of my book from her aunt in Buffalo, New York. This particular book was found on a window sill in a Tai Chi Studio in Buffalo and Meg thought she was following the instructions on the inside cover which she assumed was written by me. Except, I have no idea how that book got to Buffalo or who wrote that note. Meg and I are now fast friends and I have been to her new book club where the very first book they read was The Hundred Story Home. But it also turns out Meg is a talented coach, and I needed some coaching!

Elizabeth Osteen

Greensboro, NC

Elizabeth and I probably should have met a decade ago back when I was working with her brother, Bill Holt, raising money for Moore Place. He was the $3 million man in the book and his sister is no less remarkable. But we only connected after Elizabeth invited me to First Presbyterian Church Greensboro and then to The Reviewers—a century old book club! Wheels are now turning about turning a building at First Presbyterian into housing!

Ana Lothspeich

Charlotte, NC

Ana and I worked together almost thirty years ago at an advertising agency. When she reached out after reading the book, I was surprised to learn Ana had changed careers completely and was working at one of Charlotte’s largest catholic churches, St Gabriel, as the Pastoral Care Director. Ana invited me to speak twice, once about The Moore Place story and then, about Finding Your Calling. The conversations sparked by these talks have led this parish to study how they can advocate for affordable housing in meaningful ways and they have even recently intervened on behalf of a homeless young man who became hospitalized after a senseless beating on the streets.

Melinda Billings

Greensboro, NC

Melinda came up to me at a book signing holding the book her friend had given her. Inside was the note her friend had written to her saying that my story reminded her of Melinda’s and encouraging her to listen for her whisper. Melinda was also a graphic designer and mom, looking for purpose after a recent struggle with the death of her parents. Melinda shared at the book signing that my book, and listening led her to start a new career with a housing nonprofit in Guilford County
